rationale

Rationale page - List all the deliverables you have implemented, and and any features or improvements you have added. Justify your decisions for choosing a particular layout and design. If you used advanced technology such as jQuery or CSS3, mention where it is being used, and why. Also List any features you had planned to implement but could not complete. If your rationale does not decribe a sufficient quantity of improvements, and if it does not effectively make a case for how these changes improve the site, your client will not be conviced that your team is the best choice.

Observations

The following are some observations on the COMP1950 site:

  • There's a lot of contents making it a bit hard to read
  • A virtual area where students can actually code and practice the exercise would be nice
  • Minimize the navigation to free up space
  • Ease of access to lectures, exercises and links
  • Hide the parts that are not important
  • Better semantics for easier maintenance

Improvements

Basing off of the observations, our team has made the following improvements:

  • Converted the navigation to be on the top navigation
  • All of the exercise are linked to "Pen" in the CodePen site. Students can make the changes and see the results right away.
  • Vertical navigation for sections of the page
  • A secure area for students to login
  • Online quiz form with timer
  • Used HTML5 tags to replaced/appended current tags could be put to better use for analytics or mobile responsive
